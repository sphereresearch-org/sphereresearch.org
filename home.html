Why SPHERE?

From protest to durable participation.

SPHERE was created to prevent a slide back into civic passivity after the 2022 people’s struggle. We connect human rights advocacy with community‑level action—standing up for freedom of expression, association, and privacy while strengthening everyday democratic practice.

Legal status: Incorporation in progress as a company limited by guarantee (Section 32). Name reservation approved for SPHERE Research and Advocacy.

Advocacy

2025–2026 Priorities

Clear, achievable, impact‑driven.

  • Complete legal registration and strengthen governance.
  • Deliver Civil & Political Rights in the Digital Age and scale Our Rights – Our Future trainings.
  • Establish CDPR as Sri Lanka’s leading voice on digital rights in law and policy.
  • Run a national digital rights advocacy campaign via CDPR.

Institutional Strength

Finalize incorporation; build transparent systems; publish policies.

Public Education

Expand rights literacy and safety practices for communities and defenders.

Policy Impact

Evidence‑based advocacy shaping digital rights frameworks.

Centre for Digital Policy Research (CDPR)

Legal and policy engine for digital rights.

CDPR leads SPHERE’s legal‑policy agenda—producing research, convening stakeholders, and driving advocacy on privacy, expression, surveillance, platform accountability, and civic technology. CDPR will front the national digital rights campaign and provide rapid policy response capacity.

Focus Areas

Data protection, platform regulation, speech & assembly online, due process, transparency.

Outputs

Briefs, explainer notes, stakeholder roundtables, model clauses, media commentary.

Community Link

Bridging legal work with trainings and community needs.

Team & Structure

Lean core, networked expertise.

  • Board of Directors — Strategic oversight with an independent majority.
  • Core Team — Lean staff across advocacy, research, training, and operations.
  • Leadership — Cross‑functional co‑founders guiding programs and partnerships.
  • Advisory Board — Subject‑matter experts and legal/technical consultants engaged as needed.
  • Grassroots Network — Community partners, local organizers, and field trainers across Sri Lanka.
