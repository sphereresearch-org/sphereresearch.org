SPHERE recently concluded a comprehensive training program for community leaders from across Sri Lanka, focusing on human rights advocacy, documentation, and community mobilization. This initiative reflects our commitment to building grassroots capacity for sustained civic engagement.

## Program Overview

The five-day residential training brought together 30 community leaders from diverse backgrounds, representing urban and rural communities, different ethnic groups, and various civil society sectors. The program covered:

- Fundamental human rights principles
- Documentation methodologies
- Advocacy strategies
- Community organizing
- Digital security and online activism

## Key Learning Areas

### 1. Human Rights Framework

Participants gained understanding of:
- International human rights standards
- National legal frameworks
- Mechanisms for rights protection
- Common violations and remedies

### 2. Documentation Skills

Effective documentation is crucial for accountability. We covered:
- Interview techniques
- Evidence collection
- Digital documentation tools
- Protecting victims and witnesses
- Data security

### 3. Advocacy Strategies

Participants learned how to:
- Identify advocacy targets and objectives
- Build coalitions and networks
- Engage with media
- Use social media effectively
- Navigate policy processes

### 4. Community Mobilization

Building on participants' existing community work, we explored:
- Participatory approaches
- Inclusive decision-making
- Building trust across divides
- Sustaining momentum
- Dealing with repression

## Participant Reflections

*"This training gave me practical tools I can use immediately in my community. The documentation module was particularly valuable."* - Community leader from Jaffna

*"Meeting leaders from other communities helped me see that our struggles are connected. We're now planning joint advocacy initiatives."* - Activist from Colombo

*"The digital security session was eye-opening. I realized I was putting myself and my community at risk."* - Organizer from Kandy

## Ongoing Support

Training is just the beginning. SPHERE provides ongoing support through:

- Monthly follow-up sessions
- A peer support network
- Access to resources and tools
- Mentorship from experienced advocates
- Small grants for community initiatives

## Impact and Next Steps

Alumni of our training programs have gone on to:
- Document rights violations in their communities
- Successfully advocate for policy changes
- Build local human rights monitoring networks
- Protect vulnerable community members
- Train other community leaders

## Future Training Initiatives

Building on this success, SPHERE plans to:
- Expand training to more communities
- Develop specialized modules (e.g., land rights, women's rights)
- Create training materials in Tamil and Sinhala
- Partner with universities for certification
- Establish a training-of-trainers program
