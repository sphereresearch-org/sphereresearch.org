The 2022 Aragalaya (people's struggle) marked a watershed moment in Sri Lankan history. As millions took to the streets demanding accountability and systemic change, civil society organizations like SPHERE played a crucial role in connecting grassroots action with advocacy for lasting reforms.

## The People's Struggle

From March to July 2022, Sri Lanka witnessed unprecedented mass mobilization. Citizens from all walks of life came together to protest against economic mismanagement, corruption, and the erosion of democratic institutions. The Aragalaya was remarkable for its:

* **Inclusivity**: Cutting across ethnic, religious, and class divides
* **Creativity**: Innovative forms of protest and civic expression
* **Persistence**: Sustained mobilization over several months
* **Clear Demands**: Focused calls for accountability and systemic reform

## SPHERE's Response

During this critical period, SPHERE worked to:

### 1. Document Rights Violations

We systematically documented incidents of:

* Excessive use of force by security forces
* Restrictions on peaceful assembly
* Attacks on protesters
* Detention of activists

This documentation serves as a crucial record for accountability processes and informs our ongoing advocacy.

### 2. Provide Legal Support

Working with partner organizations, we helped:

* Connect arrested protesters with legal aid
* Monitor court proceedings
* Advocate for respect for due process rights

### 3. Amplify Voices

We used our platforms to:

* Share protesters' demands and narratives
* Counter misinformation
* Provide analysis of developments
* Connect local movements with international solidarity

### 4. Bridge Grassroots and Policy

We worked to ensure that the energy of street protests translated into concrete policy demands:

* Constitutional reforms
* Anti-corruption measures
* Economic restructuring with social protection
* Accountability for past abuses

## Lessons Learned

The Aragalaya taught us valuable lessons about civic participation and advocacy:

### Sustained Engagement is Critical

While mass mobilization captured attention and created pressure for change, transforming that momentum into lasting reforms requires sustained engagement. SPHERE's approach focuses on:

* Building institutional capacity for long-term advocacy
* Developing policy expertise to inform reform processes
* Maintaining connections with grassroots movements
* Creating spaces for continued civic participation

### Digital Tools Matter

Digital platforms played a crucial role in the Aragalaya:

* Organizing and coordinating actions
* Sharing information in real-time
* Countering official narratives
* Building solidarity across communities

However, this also highlighted the need for:

* Digital security awareness
* Media literacy
* Protection against online harassment
* Access for digitally excluded communities

### Cross-Movement Solidarity

The Aragalaya demonstrated the power of solidarity across different movements and communities. Building on this, SPHERE works to:

* Connect human rights, economic justice, and environmental movements
* Bridge ethnic and religious divides
* Include marginalized voices in decision-making
* Foster a culture of mutual support among activists

## Current Challenges

While the Aragalaya achieved some immediate objectives, significant challenges remain:

### Backlash and Repression

Authorities have responded to mass mobilization with:

* Arrests and prosecutions of protest leaders
* Restrictions on assembly rights
* Increased surveillance
* Efforts to delegitimize civic activism

### Economic Crisis

The underlying economic crisis continues, with:

* Unprecedented inflation
* Food and fuel insecurity
* Increased poverty and inequality
* Brain drain as skilled workers emigrate

### Reform Stagnation

Progress on demanded reforms has been slow:

* Limited accountability for economic mismanagement
* Incomplete constitutional reforms
* Weak anti-corruption measures
* Insufficient economic restructuring

## The Path Forward

SPHERE's work is guided by the belief that the Aragalaya's spirit of civic participation must be sustained and institutionalized. Our approach includes:

### 1. Strengthening Civic Infrastructure

* Building the capacity of civil society organizations
* Creating networks for sustained advocacy
* Developing leadership among younger activists
* Ensuring adequate resources for civic action

### 2. Policy Advocacy

* Pushing for concrete implementation of demanded reforms
* Engaging with policy processes
* Providing technical expertise
* Monitoring government commitments

### 3. Documentation and Accountability

* Maintaining comprehensive records of rights violations
* Supporting transitional justice processes
* Advocating for accountability mechanisms
* Preserving the memory of civic resistance

### 4. Inclusive Participation

* Ensuring marginalized voices are heard
* Breaking down barriers to civic engagement
* Building trust across communities
* Fostering a culture of democratic participation

## Conclusion

The 2022 Aragalaya showed that ordinary citizens, when united, can challenge entrenched power structures and demand change. SPHERE's mission is to ensure that this spirit of civic participation translates into lasting democratic transformation.

We remain committed to working alongside communities, movements, and individuals to build a Sri Lanka that respects human rights, ensures accountability, and creates opportunities for all its citizens.
