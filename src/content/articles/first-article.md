# Digital Rights in Sri Lanka: 2024 Policy Brief

As Sri Lanka continues to navigate rapid digital transformation, the intersection of technology and human rights has never been more critical. This policy brief, authored by the Centre for Digital Policy Research (CDPR) team, outlines key areas of concern and strategic recommendations for 2024.

## Key Focus Areas

1.  **Freedom of Expression Online:** We examine the impact of recent legislative frameworks on digital free speech and propose safeguards against arbitrary censorship.
2.  **Data Protection:** With the increasing digitalization of both public and private sector services, robust data protection mechanisms are urgently needed to secure citizen privacy.
3.  **Digital Inclusion:** Addressing the digital divide remains a priority, ensuring that marginalized communities have equitable access to technology and digital literacy programs.

## Strategic Recommendations

The CDPR strongly advocates for a multi-stakeholder approach to digital governance, involving civil society, government bodies, and technology providers. By fostering transparent dialogue, we can build a digital ecosystem that respects fundamental human rights while driving sustainable progress.
